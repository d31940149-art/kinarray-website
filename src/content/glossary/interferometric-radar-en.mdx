---
term: "Interferometric Radar"
definition: "A radar technique that measures distance changes by comparing the phase of radar signals over time, enabling millimeter-level accuracy in deformation monitoring."
lang: "en"
relatedArticles: ["deformation-radar-technology-en"]
---

Interferometric radar, also known as InSAR (Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar), is the core technology behind modern deformation monitoring systems. By analyzing the phase difference between successive radar measurements, it can detect movements as small as 0.1mm.
